Election 2020

So far 4,462 people have voted early in Minnesota's presidential primary. Here's what to watch.

By Jeff Hargarten
Star Tribune Updated January 27, 2020

Thousands of Minnesotans have already voted in the state's first presential primary in decades, which is one among several Democratic nomination contests slated for Super Tuesday this coming March 3.

Polls have been open to absentee voters since Jan. 17 for the primaries, both via mail and in person at various locations. More voters are expected to participate in this primary over the course of weeks, compared to the previous Minnesota caucuses that were only held during single evenings.

Since this is Minnesota's first presidential primary with early voting, it's not directly comparable to turnout in past elections. Whether concerns over voter privacy in a partisan primary affect turnout remains to be seen.

This page will be updated as more data on geographic and turnout trends are made available by the Secretary of State.

How many Minnesotans are voting early?

Minnesota has allowed early voting since 2014, with 2016 and 2018 seeing a surge of absentee balloting as the practice becomes more commonplace.

After one week of voting, 4,462 Minnesotans had cast ballots in the presidential primary. For context, that's more accepted early ballots after one week than in 2014's midterm election, a testament to advanced voting's increased adoption.

Previous years of early voting tallies in general elections are included for reference rather than direct comparison.

Minnesota often leads the nation in voter turnout, and the U.S. Elections Project shows advanced voting isn't usually much different.
